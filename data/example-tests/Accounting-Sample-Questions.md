# Accounting Sample Questions

FBLA Accounting Sample Questions

Disclaimer: These are sample test questions of what a competitor will find in this competition.
To view the official study guide, with the knowledge area and objective list, visit the guidelines
on the Competitive Events page of the national website at www.fbla.org.

## Question 1

What is the purpose of journalizing in accounting?
A. To calculate tax rates
B. To track customer feedback
C. To record business transactions in chronological order
D. To create advertisements

## Question 2

A. Accounts Payable
B. Building
C. Owner’s Equity
D. Revenue

## Question 3

A. Assets = Liabilities - Owner’s Equity
B. Assets = Liabilities + Owner’s Equity
C. Liabilities = Assets + Owner’s Equity
D. Owner’s Equity = Revenue - Expenses

A. Balance Sheet
B. Statement of Cash Flows
C. Income Statement
D. Statement of Owner’s Equity

## Question 5

A. To show cash inflows and outflows
B. To report the financial position of a business at a point in time
C. To list sales transactions
D. To detail payroll deductions

## Question 6

Which type of account is 'Accounts Receivable'?
A. Liability
B. Asset
C. Revenue
D. Expense

## Question 7

In double-entry accounting, every transaction affects how many accounts?
A. One
B. Two or more
C. None
D. All accounts

## Question 8

A. Cash
B. Supplies
C. Capital
D. Prepaid Insurance

## Question 9

A. Initial recording of transactions
B. Adjusting entries
C. Summarized account balances
D. Payroll calculation

## Question 10

A. Revenue
B. Accounts Payable
C. Equipment
D. Capital

## Question 11

A. To create journal entries
B. To prepare payroll
C. To organize data for financial statements
D. To monitor stock prices

A. Ledger
B. Trial Balance
C. Bank Reconciliation
D. Invoice

B. A record of employee pay details
C. A list of all assets

A. A company owned by shareholders
B. A government agency
C. A business owned by one person
D. A nonprofit organization

## Question 15

A. An increase in asset value
B. A reduction in liabilities
C. The expense of using long-term assets
D. Money lost to theft

## Question 16

A. Equipment
B. Trademark
C. Inventory
D. Vehicle

## Question 17

How is net income calculated?
A. Assets + Liabilities
B. Revenue - Expenses
C. Revenue + Capital
D. Assets - Revenue

## Question 18

What does the term 'liability' refer to?
A. A company’s investment
B. Property owned
C. Amounts owed to others
D. Revenue from sales

## Question 19

What does GAAP stand for?
A. General Asset Allocation Principles
B. Government Authorized Accounting Practices
C. Generally Accepted Accounting Principles
D. General Accounting Asset Policies

## Question 20

A. Balance Sheet
B. Trial Balance
C. Income Statement
D. Statement of Owner's Equity

## Question 21

A. Asset
B. Liability
C. Expense
D. Revenue

## Question 22

A. To delete old accounts
B. To fix errors
C. To update account balances before financial statements
D. To calculate interest

## Question 23

What is a trial balance used for?
A. To test bank security
B. To list all journal entries
C. To ensure debits equal credits
D. To show net income

## Question 24

A. Recording revenue before it's earned
B. Matching expenses with revenues in the same period
C. Matching assets with liabilities
D. Posting entries twice

## Question 25

A. Posting, Journalizing, Analyzing, Financial Statements
B. Analyzing, Journalizing, Posting, Trial Balance, Adjusting Entries, Financial Statements
C. Financial Statements, Posting, Journalizing
D. Adjusting Entries, Trial Balance, Journalizing

## Question 26

What is a petty cash fund used for?
A. Paying salaries
B. Large purchases
C. Small, routine business expenses
D. Depositing checks

## Question 27

How are expenses recorded in the accounting equation?
A. They increase assets
B. They decrease liabilities
C. They decrease owner’s equity
D. They increase revenue

## Question 28

What is the purpose of closing entries?
A. To prepare permanent accounts for the next period
B. To correct errors
C. To adjust asset accounts
D. To calculate payroll

## Question 29

What does 'accrued expense' mean?
A. An expense paid in advance
B. A recorded liability that has not been paid yet
C. A prepaid asset
D. Revenue earned but not collected

## Question 30

Which of the following is considered an operating activity in the statement of cash flows?
A. Buying equipment
B. Borrowing money
C. Selling products
D. Paying dividends

## Question 31

Which of the following best describes internal controls in accounting?
A. Methods to increase revenue
B. Procedures to ensure accuracy and prevent fraud
C. Techniques for tax evasion
D. Strategies for investment

## Question 32

What is a contra asset account?
A. An account that increases liabilities
B. An account that offsets an asset account
C. An account that tracks equity
D. An account that doubles income

## Question 33

What does the term 'fiscal year' refer to?
A. A calendar year
B. The period from January to December
C. A 12-month period used for financial reporting
D. A tax refund schedule

## Question 34

A. Revenue
B. Contra revenue
C. Expense
D. Asset

## Question 35

How are prepaid expenses recorded on the balance sheet?
A. As liabilities
B. As owner's equity
C. As assets
D. As expenses

## Question 36

What is a common depreciation method used in accounting?
A. Amortization
B. Sum-of-the-hours method
C. Straight-line method
D. Asset expansion

## Question 37

A. To verify the general ledger
B. To check balances after closing entries are made
C. To record new transactions
D. To report income

## Question 38

Which of the following is a temporary account?
A. Accounts Payable
B. Owner’s Capital
C. Rent Expense
D. Equipment

## Question 39

What is the effect of an adjusting entry for accrued revenue?
A. Decrease an asset and increase a liability
B. Increase a liability and revenue
C. Increase an asset and increase revenue
D. Decrease revenue and increase equity

## Question 40

Which document provides a record of all business transactions in chronological order?
A. Ledger
B. Journal
C. Invoice
D. Income Statement

## Question 41

What is the purpose of the income summary account?
A. To summarize changes in cash
B. To transfer net income or loss to capital
C. To list all owner withdrawals
D. To compute gross profit

## Question 42

When preparing a bank reconciliation, which item would be subtracted from the book
balance?
A. Deposits in transit
B. Outstanding checks
C. Bank service charges
D. Cash on hand

## Question 43

A. Balance Sheet
B. Ledger
C. Income Statement
D. Trial Balance

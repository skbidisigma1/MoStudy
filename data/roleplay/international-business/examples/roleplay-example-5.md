**COMPETITOR INSTRUCTIONS**

1.  This is an interactive, role-playing event.

2.  You have **20 minutes** to review the role play scenario and prepare
    for the role play.

3.  Presentation time is **seven minutes**. Competitors will be given a
    one-minute warning indicated by the timekeeper standing and holding
    up a colored card. At seven minutes the timekeeper will stand and
    hold up a colored card indicating time is up. Competitors must stop
    when time is up.

4.  The presentation is an interactive role play with the judges who
    will ask questions.

5.  Each competitor will be given two note cards to make notes. Note
    cards can be used during the role play. Note cards will be collected
    following the role play.

6.  Cover all the points described in the role play.

7.  If competing as a team, all competitors must participate in the role
    play as well as answer the questions posed by the judges.

**ROLE PLAY SCENARIO**

<table>
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><p><strong><u>Background</u></strong></p>
<p>You are the co-founder of a small, community-based business in India.
You and your partner source fragrant spices directly from local farmers,
package them using sustainable materials, and sell them at local markets
and through your online store. Your unique blends and commitment to fair
trade practices have garnered a loyal following.</p>
<p><strong><u>Scenario</u></strong></p>
<p>A renowned European chef and restaurateur has discovered your spices
at a food festival and proposes an exciting opportunity. He wants to
feature your spices in his restaurants across Europe and potentially in
his upcoming cookbook. This could propel your business to international
fame and significantly boost your income. However, venturing into the
European market requires careful planning and execution. What decision
will you make and why? You will be explaining it to the management team
at your company.</p></td>
</tr>
